Project Blueprint: Digital Msharika (KKKT-DMP)
Target: Evangelical Lutheran Church in Tanzania (East & Coastal Diocese)
Fiscal Year: 2026 Strategy
Author: Gemini AI / Project Lead
1. Executive Summary
The Digital Msharika Project aims to digitize the manual "Census and Pledge" forms used by the KKKT-DMP. By migrating from paper to a relational database, the church will gain actionable insights into its congregation's demographics, professional skills, and spiritual growth, while providing members with a modern, engaging interface for daily church life.
2. Problem Statement
The current manual system (Page 1 & 2 of the Parish Form) suffers from:
* Data Inaccessibility: Records are physically stored, making it impossible to search for professional skills or demographic trends.
* Disconnected Members: Lack of a feedback loop regarding spiritual milestones or fellowship activities.
* Engagement Gap: Members only interact with church administration during Sunday services or office hours.
* Financial Ambiguity: Manual tracking of 2026 Pledges (Ahadi) is prone to error and lacks transparency for the member.
3. Database Architecture (ERD Mapping)
The database is structured to mirror the 100+ data points across the two uploaded form pages.
Core Tables & Relationships
A. Member Profile (Section A & B)
Maps to the primary personal and contact fields.
* member_id (PK)
* membership_no (Unique Namba ya Ahadi)
* full_name, gender, dob, marital_status
* marriage_details (Type, Date, Location, Spouse Name)
* contact_details (Primary Phone, Email, Residence Area, House/Block No)
* church_hierarchy (Previous Parish, Elder Name, Elder Phone)
B. Spiritual & Professional Progress (Section C & D)
Tracks the "Journey of Faith" and the "Marketplace Skills."
* is_baptized (Boolean) & baptism_date
* is_confirmed (Boolean) & confirmation_date
* takes_communion (Boolean)
* profession & education_level
* occupation & work_place
* volunteer_status (Willingness to serve)
C. Fellowship & Group Participation (Section E)
* fellowship_id (FK to a Master Fellowships table)
* group_flags (Individual Booleans for: Choir Kuu, Uinjilisti, Tarumbeta, Sunday School, Women’s Union, Youth Union, etc.)
* fellowship_leader_info
D. Financial Pledges (Section F)
* building_pledge_2026 (Decimal)
* stewardship_pledge_2026 (Decimal)
* other_pledges (Decimal)
* submission_date
4. Key Components & Features
Feature 1: The Spiritual Roadmap (User Dashboard)
* Visual Milestones: A "Progress Bar" showing icons for Baptism, Confirmation, and Choir Membership. Unlocked milestones appear in color; pending ones are grayscale.
* Digital ID: A QR-code enabled digital membership card using the member's photo and registration number.
Feature 2: Fellowship Finder (Community Link)
* Smart Matching: Uses the member's "Residence Area" to automatically suggest the nearest Nyumba kwa Nyumba (House-to-House) group.
* Leadership Access: One-tap calling/messaging for the assigned Church Elder (Mzee wa Kanisa) or Fellowship Chairperson.
Feature 3: The Parish Feed (Daily Engagement)
* Daily Liturgy: Digital access to the day's readings, order of service, and hymns.
* Matangazo (Announcements): A real-time feed for Wedding Bans, Funerals, and General Parish news, categorized for easy filtering.
* Verse of the Day: Automated daily bread notifications sent by the Admin.
Feature 4: Skill-Based Marketplace
* Internal Networking: A directory allowing members to find service providers (e.g., "Find a Mechanic in our Parish") based on the profession field captured in the form.
5. Technical Stack & Scalability
* Database: PostgreSQL (for relational integrity)
* Backend: Node.js / Python (FastAPI)
* Frontend: React Native (to target both Android and iOS users in Tanzania)
* International Readiness: - Localization: Language files for Swahili, English, and German.
   * Multi-Tenant: Architecture allows other KKKT Dioceses or different denominations (e.g., Catholic/Anglican) to use the same system with custom fields.
6. Implementation Roadmap
1. Phase 1: Database migration and Admin Data Entry Portal.
2. Phase 2: Launch Member Dashboard and Fellowship Finder.
3. Phase 3: Integrate Daily Liturgy and Push Notifications.
4. Phase 4: Expand to Diocese-wide analytics for Bishops and District Pastors.
